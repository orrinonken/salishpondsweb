About Orrin

Orrin Onken is a retired elder‑law attorney from Portland, Oregon. His fiction features Leopold Larson, a sober, sharp‑witted lawyer who knows the justice system from the inside; his nonfiction gathers generous, funny essays about growing older and living well.

He has written extensively about aging on Medium and authored two legal mysteries alongside other works of fiction. Off the page, he bakes bread and walks his dog, Bob, around the ponds near his home.

Why readers love his work

  • Authenticity: a career in elder law and a candid recovery journey inform the details and the humor.
  • Heart and wit: essays that entertain while slipping in useful wisdom about aging.
  • Legal stakes: mysteries that balance high tension with humane, street‑level insight.
